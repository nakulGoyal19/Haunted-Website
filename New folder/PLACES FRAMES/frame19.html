Story Behind the MALCHA MAHAL

MALCHA MAHAL, also known as Wilayat Mahal, is a Tughlak era hunting lodge in the Chanakyapuri area of New Delhi,
India next to the Delhi Earth Station of the Indian Space Research Organisation.[1][2] It was built by Firuz Shah Tughlaq,
who reigned over the Sultanate of Delhi, in 1325.[3] It was renamed to Wilayat Mahal after Begum Wilayat Mahal of Awadh
who was reportedly given the place by the Government of India in May 1985.[1][4][5] On 10 September 1993, Begum committed
suicide at the age of 62 by consuming crushed diamonds.[6] The building is now mostly in ruins, though it is still inhabited by the Begum's daughter Sakina Mahal, and son Prince Ali Raza.
